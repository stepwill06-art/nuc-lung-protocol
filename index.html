Lung V/Q Imaging Protocol

Comprehensive guide for patients and healthcare professionals

❓ What Is This Test?

This lung imaging test uses a special camera to take pictures of your lungs and see how well they're working. It has two parts:

Part 1: Breathing Test (5-10 minutes)

You'll breathe in a harmless mist through a mouthpiece. This mist helps the camera see how air moves into different parts of your lungs.

Part 2: Injection Test (30-40 minutes)

A small amount of radioactive dye will be injected into your arm through an IV. This dye flows through your blood to your lungs, and the special camera takes pictures.

Total Time: About 45 minutes

🏥 Why Do I Need This Test?

Your doctor ordered this test to check for:

  • Blood clots in the lungs (pulmonary embolism)
  • Breathing problems caused by lung disease
  • Areas of your lungs not getting enough blood flow
  • Other lung conditions that affect breathing and blood circulation
This test is one of the safest ways to evaluate how your lungs are working.

✋ How to Prepare

📋

Before Your Visit

Tell your doctor if you're pregnant or breastfeeding. Bring your insurance card and ID. Arrive 15 minutes early.

👕

What to Wear

Comfortable, loose-fitting clothes. Avoid metal buttons and zippers. Remove all jewelry.

🍽️

Food & Drink

You can eat and drink normally. No fasting required. Have a regular breakfast if your appointment is in the morning.

⏱️ What Happens During Your Test

Check-In (10 min)

Sign in and complete a brief health questionnaire.

IV Placement (5 min)

A nurse places a small IV in your arm. You may feel a brief pinch.

Breathing Test (5-10 min)

Breathe in mist through a mouthpiece. Take a deep breath and hold briefly.

Injection (5 min)

Radioactive dye is injected through your IV. You may feel slight warmth.

Imaging (20-30 min)

Camera rotates around you taking pictures. Stay still and hold your breath when instructed.

Finish (5 min)

IV line is removed. You can go home immediately. No special care needed.

🛡️ Safety & Radiation

Yes, this is safe. This test has been used worldwide for decades. Millions of patients have had this procedure without problems.

Radiation Exposure

The radiation dose from this test is very low—similar to natural radiation you'd receive in about 2 years from your environment.

After Your Test

  • You can go home right away—no restrictions
  • No special care needed
  • You can drive, work, and eat normally
  • Resume all normal activities
Hydration tip: Drink plenty of water to help flush the dye from your body. It will leave naturally through urine in 24 hours.

❔ Frequently Asked Questions

Will the test hurt? ▼
No. The only thing you might feel is a brief pinch when the IV is placed. The breathing part and imaging are completely painless.
Can I eat or drink before the test? ▼
Yes. You can eat and drink normally. There's no fasting required.
Will I be radioactive after the test? ▼
No. The tiny amount of dye is quickly cleared from your body. It's safe to be around family and friends immediately.
How long will it take? ▼
Plan for about one hour total. The actual imaging takes about 30-35 minutes.

📋 Clinical Overview

Ventilation/Perfusion (V/Q) imaging is a nuclear medicine procedure used to evaluate lung ventilation and perfusion in patients with suspected pulmonary embolism (PE), chronic lung disease, and other respiratory conditions.

Clinical Significance

  • Assesses regional lung ventilation and blood perfusion
  • Gold standard for PE diagnosis in patients with normal chest X-ray
  • Evaluates chronic lung disease, asthma, and COPD
  • Uses low radiation dose with Tc-99m radiopharmaceuticals
🧪

Pre-Procedure

Patient assessment, chest X-ray, informed consent, IV line placement

💨

Ventilation Phase

Aerosol inhalation via nebulizer, posterior imaging

🩸

Perfusion Phase

IV radiopharmaceutical injection, multi-angle imaging

🏥 Pre-Procedure Preparation

Patient Assessment

Risk Stratification:
  • Evaluate pre-test probability using clinical criteria (Wells Score, D-dimer)
  • Determine if low, intermediate, or high probability for PE
  • Review contraindications and allergies

Baseline Imaging

Obtain PA and lateral chest X-ray within 1 hour. Essential for V/Q interpretation.

Patient Preparation

  • Remove metallic objects and jewelry
  • Explain procedure and obtain informed consent
  • Establish IV access for radiopharmaceutical injection
  • Position patient supine or seated comfortably

💨 Ventilation Phase

Radiopharmaceutical

Tc-99m DTPA Aerosol
Activity: 25-40 mCi (0.925-1.48 GBq)
Route: Inhalation via nebulizer
Purpose: Assesses regional ventilation

Imaging Technique

  • Position patient upright in front of gamma camera
  • Anterior view preferred (posterior acceptable)
  • Ensure lungs are centered in camera field of view

Administration Procedure

  1. Instruct patient: "Take a deep breath and exhale normally"
  2. Introduce aerosol mist into breathing system
  3. Instruct patient: "Continue breathing the mist. Take a deep breath and hold it."
  4. Obtain anterior image during breath hold (10-20 seconds)
  5. Allow patient to breathe normally
  6. Acquire additional projections as per protocol
⚠️ Critical: Ensure adequate breath hold for sharp, diagnostic images. Coaching patient improves image quality.

💉 Perfusion Phase

Radiopharmaceutical

Tc-99m MAA (Macroaggregated Albumin)
Activity: 5-10 mCi (0.185-0.37 GBq)
Route: Intravenous injection
Purpose: Assesses pulmonary perfusion

Pre-Injection Preparation

  • Position patient supine on imaging table
  • Ensure IV line is patent and functioning
⚠️ Critical: Patient MUST be supine during MAA injection to ensure uniform distribution.

Injection Technique

  1. Inject Tc-99m MAA slowly over 3-5 seconds while flushing
  2. Begin imaging immediately after injection
  3. Complete first image within 30 seconds of injection

Image Acquisition Protocol

Acquire eight static images at different angles:

View Angle Duration Counts
Anterior 0° 15-30 sec 500K
RAO/LAO 45°/315° 15-30 sec 500K
RL/LL 90°/270° 15-30 sec 500K
RPO/LPO 135°/225° 15-30 sec 500K
Posterior 180° 15-30 sec 500K

⚙️ Equipment & Materials

Required Equipment

📷

Gamma Camera

Dual-head or multi-head system with low-energy, high-resolution collimator

🌫️

Aerosol Nebulizer

Disposable system compatible with Tc-99m DTPA

💉

IV Equipment

18-20 gauge catheter, normal saline flush, tourniquet

Radiopharmaceuticals

Agent Activity Route Purpose Half-Life
Tc-99m DTPA Aerosol 25-40 mCi Inhalation Ventilation imaging 6 hours
Tc-99m MAA 5-10 mCi IV injection Perfusion imaging 6 hours
